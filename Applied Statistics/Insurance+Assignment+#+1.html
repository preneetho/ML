#function to find outliers

def findOutliers(column):
    anomalies = []
    #Find Q1
    Q1 = column.quantile(0.25)
    #Find Q3
    Q3 = column.quantile(0.75)
    #Find IQR
    IQR = Q3-Q1
    
    #Calculate lower and upper limits
    lower_limit  = Q1 - (IQR *1.5)
    upper_limit =  Q3 + (IQR *1.5)
    np.set_printoptions(precision=3)
    # Generate outliers
    for outlier in column:
        if outlier > upper_limit or outlier < lower_limit:
            anomalies.append(round(outlier,2))
    return np.array(anomalies)

Hypothesis Testing¶

4a: Do charges of people who smoke differ significantly from the people who don't?.¶

Test whether the means of two independent samples are significantly different using 2-sample t-test.

Step-1: Set up Hypothesis (NULL and Alternate)

$H_0$: Mean of charges on smokers is equal to Mean of charges on non-somker. --> $\mu1$ = $\mu2$
$H_A$: Mean of charges on smokers is not equal to Mean of charges on non-somker --> $\mu1$ <> $\mu2$

Here $\mu1$, $\mu2$ are mean of insurance charges of smokers and non-smokers respectively

Step-2: Set the Criteria for decision.

Test the hyposthesis at 5% level of significance. Since it is 2 tailed, singnificance level will be 2.5% on both sides.

Step-3: Identify the test statistic.

Here we are comparing 2 independent sample variable, hence will go with 2-sample t-test

Step-4: Compute the random chance of probability.

Test whether distribution of bmi across women with no children, one child and two children, the vary using One Way Anova test.

Step-1: Set up Hypothesis (NULL and Alternate)

  • $H_0$: $\mu1$ = $\mu2$ = $\mu3$
  • $H_A$: At least one $\mu$ differs

Here $\mu1$, $\mu2$ and $\mu3$ are the mean bmi of females with 0,1, 2 children.

Step-2: Set the Criteria for decision.

Test the hyposthesis at 5% level of significance.

Step-3: Identify the test statistic.

Here we have three groups. Analysis of variance can determine whether the means of three or more groups are different. ANOVA uses F-tests to statistically test the equality of means. The test method is One Way Anova test

Step-4: Compute the random chance of probability using ANOVA table.
